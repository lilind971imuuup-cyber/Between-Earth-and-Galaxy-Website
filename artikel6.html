The Art of Slow Living: Finding Peace Through Caring for Houseplants

February 12, 2026  |  Stargazing & Gardening

Stargazers, get your telescopes ready! February 2026 is bringing us a breathtaking celestial spectacle. In a rare cosmic event, six major planets are lining up in the night sky, creating a stunning "Planet Parade" that connects our Earth to the wider galaxy.

This alignment features Mercury, Venus, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, and Uranus dancing across the horizon. While planet alignments happen every few years, having six of them visible around the same time is an extraordinary event you won't want to miss. Here is everything you need to know about the planets joining the parade:

1. Moonflower (Ipomoea alba)r

A magical vine with large, pure white blossoms that unfurl only at dusk. Under the moonlight, these flowers look like glowing cosmic rings, releasing a sweet fragrance into the night air.

2. Evening Primrose (Oenothera biennis)

Venus will outshine almost everything else near the horizon. Known for its thick, reflective cloud layer, it will appear as an incredibly bright beacon just before sunrise or right after sunset.

3. Mars — The Rusty Neighbor

Look closely for a distinct reddish-orange tint. Mars won't be as bright as Jupiter or Venus during this parade, but its unique fiery color makes it instantly recognizable among the stars.

4. Saturn — The Ringed Jewel

Saturn emits a calm, yellowish glow. While you can easily see the planet itself without help, bringing a small pair of binoculars or a basic telescope will reveal its majestic ring system.

5. Mercury — The Swift Messenger

Mercury is always the trickiest to spot because it stays very close to the Sun. You will have a narrow window of time to catch it low on the horizon just as the twilight fades.

6. Uranus — The Distant Ice Giant

Unlike the others, Uranus is too far away to see with just your eyes. To officially complete your six-planet checklist, you will need a telescope and a stargazing app to pinpoint its faint, pale-blue dot.

Remember, the golden rule for planet hunting is to find a spot away from bright city lights and look for a clear, open horizon. Clear skies, and happy stargazing across the galaxy!
